Parts index

Here's what you'll find in our weekly workshops kits.

Parts marked in red are not part of our standard parts, but they're still open to borrow for experienced members!

Controllers

Arduino nano

An arduino nano is a small microprocessor that is the brains of many a robotics project. We also have peripherals for our Arduino nanos, so don't worry about the cabling!

ESP32-cam

The ESP32-cam combines a wireless chip, camera and SD card reader along with output ports. However, it operates at 3.3V and is somewhat more expensive than an arduino.

Raspberry Pi 3B+

This board is a full-flegded (albeit less powerful) mini computer for heavy processing requirements. You could make a server on wheels!

Miscellaneous

Resistors

Resistors are good for tuning the input of analogue signals and regulating diode power.

Breadboard

You put robotics components on a breadboard so you can link them together.

Jumper wires

Jumper wires help link components together. They come in male-male, male-female and female-female variants.

Push buttons

Push to complete the circuit! Make sure to protect with a resistor.

LEDs

These light up when you put current through them the right way. We've got red ones and green ones. Make sure you protect them with a resistor!.

Sensors

Ultrasonic sensor

An ultrasonic sensor allows a robot to measure the range to an object directly in front of it. It's not very precise, but it's a very cheap sensor.

Laser rangefinder

Performs the same task as the ultrasound, but with a narrower beam spread so it's more focused. It's also somewhat more expensive.

Inertial measurement unit

This unit figures out where your robot is in space, by measuring its acceleration, rotation and compass bearing. It's not always super precise though...

GPS

These hobby grade GPS sensors will locate your robot to the nearest 10 metres on the surface of the earth - which is both really good and really bad, depending on how you think about it.

Actuators

Servo

A servo gives a robot precise movement. However, our small servos only have a limited range of motion (180 degrees).

Motor

A motor has an unlimited range of motion but consumes a lot more power and is much less precise. It's great for wheels.

Stepper motor

A stepper motor is both powerful and precise, but requires some additional processing power to keep it going.

Racecar Chassis

Definitely a step up from the others. This is a mainstay of our DRC teams, but while they're off season we let you have a go with them!
